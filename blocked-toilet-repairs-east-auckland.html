Blocked Toilet Repairs & unblocking in East Auckland

Blocked toilets are a serious menace that needs quick repair or replacement. If left for a long time, it can cause the formation of molds and mildews which is extremely hazardous for your health as well as home or commercial building. So you have to act fast and contact plumbing professionals at the earliest.

What causes Blocked Toilets?

1. Flushing non-flushable things

One of the main causes of blocked toilets is the flushing of items like sanitary napkins, cotton balls, and diapers. Sometimes accidentally flushing toys or other things thrown in by children can also lead to blockages.

2. Blocked S-trap

S-trap, which is designed to block the sewer gases from the drain, develops blockages due to the accumulation of non-degradable items being flushed.

3. Flushing too much toilet paper

Flushing many toilet papers at one go may lead to clogging at the drainage.

4. Hard water

Hard water consists of high amounts of calcium and mineral content which leaves white particles in the pipe that are hard to remove.

5. Issue in the sewer line

If there are trees near the drainage, a blockage may be caused by the invasion of tree roots causing cracks in the pipeline.

6. Old pipes

Old and rusty pipes can cause pipes to collapse resulting in the backlash of sediments.
For all these plumbing emergencies, contact our team at East Auckland Plumber who are well experienced in handling such situations.

Signs of Blocked Toilets

You must be aware of these signs and act fast by calling your plumber right away.

  • Slow draining water
  • Full or overflowing toilet bowl
  • Foul odor
  • Gurgling sound from showers and pipes
  • Water pooling around the toilet

Recommendations to Avoid Blocked Toilets

  • To keep your toilets flowing freely, never flush anything other than toilet paper. Watch your kids and tell them not to dump any toys or household things that lead to blockage.
  • Even though toilet papers are water-soluble, it is not recommended to flush all the used toilet paper at once.
  • To resolve the issue regarding hard water, you may have to install a water softener to prevent the build-up of calcium and minerals in your drainage.
  • Replace the old pipelines with the new ones before it starts to leak and collapse.
  • Have your toilet checked by professional plumbing experts, if you notice anything unusual with the toilet or the drainage system.
